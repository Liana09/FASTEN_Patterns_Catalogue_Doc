Why are safety case patterns important?

To argue about system safety, information about the system, its environment and the development processes are
structured in the form of an argumentation that represents a safety case. A safety case is documented in the form of a structured argumentation that clearly communicates that a system is acceptably safe to operate in a given context [Kelly, 1998]. As the development of safety cases poses a significant challenge due to their complexity, the concept of safety case patterns has received increasing interest from engineers, as means to reuse of successful argumentation structures.
